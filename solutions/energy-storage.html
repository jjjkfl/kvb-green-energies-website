Storage Technologies

Advanced storage solutions for thermal and electrical energy

Thermal Energy Storage (TES)

Store excess solar heat in specialized fluids or phase change materials (PCM) for use during non-sunny hours.

  • Medium: Therminol / Molten Salts / PCM
  • Retention: Up to 24 hours
  • Efficiency: >95% thermal retention
  • Temperature: Up to 400°C
  • Applications: Process heating, steam generation
Learn More

Electrical Storage (BESS)

Lithium-ion and Flow battery solutions for grid balancing and renewable integration.

  • Capacity: Scalable (kWh to MWh)
  • Response: Millisecond response time
  • Life: >5000 cycles
  • Efficiency: 92-96% round-trip
  • Applications: Backup power, grid services
Learn More

Sand Battery Storage

Innovative low-cost thermal energy storage using sand as storage medium.

  • Medium: High-grade silica sand
  • Storage: 12-48 hour retention
  • Temperature: Up to 600°C
  • Cost: Extremely economical
  • Applications: District heating, industry
Learn More

How Energy Storage Works

Four-step process for continuous energy supply

1

Energy Capture

Excess solar/wind energy is captured during peak production hours

2

Storage

Energy is stored in thermal or battery systems at optimal conditions

3

Retention

Stored energy is preserved with minimal losses (hours to days)

4

Dispatch

Stored energy is released when needed for continuous operation

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the typical lifespan of energy storage systems?

Thermal storage systems: 20+ years, Battery systems: 10-15 years, Sand battery: 25+ years. Regular maintenance can extend these lifespans.

How much space is required for installation?

Space requirements vary by capacity. Typically, 10-50 sq.m for commercial systems. We conduct site surveys to determine exact requirements.

Are there government subsidies available?

Yes, various state and central government schemes offer subsidies for energy storage systems. We assist with subsidy applications and documentation.

What maintenance is required?

Battery systems: Quarterly inspection, Thermal systems: Annual maintenance, Monitoring: Continuous remote monitoring included.
