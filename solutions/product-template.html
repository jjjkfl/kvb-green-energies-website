The Problem

  • High operational costs
  • Increasing carbon emissions

Our Solution

Our solar steam system generates renewable thermal energy using concentrated solar technology:

  • Reduces fuel consumption by up to 70%
  • Zero operational emissions
  • Stable long-term costs
  • Reliable automated operation
  • Hybrid backup for continuous service

How It Works

Four-step process for clean, efficient cooking

1

Solar Concentration

Sunlight is concentrated through precision reflectors onto a receiver

2

Heat Generation

Thermal energy heats water to generate pressurized steam (120-180°C)

3

Steam Delivery

Steam is delivered through insulated pipes to cooking vessels

4

Hybrid Operation

Automated backup ensures uninterrupted cooking during cloudy periods

Solar Reflectors

Precision-engineered mirrors concentrate sunlight 100x

Heat Receiver

Absorbs concentrated solar energy at 400-500°C

Steam Generation

Water converted to steam at 120-180°C pressure

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the typical installation time?

Installation typically takes 4-8 weeks depending on system size and site conditions. This includes site preparation, foundation work, assembly, and commissioning.

How does the system work during monsoon/cloudy days?

The system includes an automated hybrid backup (LPG/electric) that seamlessly takes over during insufficient sunlight. On partially cloudy days, the system continues to operate at reduced capacity.

What maintenance is required?

Daily: Mirror cleaning
Weekly: System check
Monthly: Mechanical inspection
Annual: Comprehensive maintenance by KVB technicians. We provide AMC packages.

What is the expected lifespan?

The structural components are designed for 25+ years. Mirrors may need replacement after 10-15 years. Automation components typically last 8-10 years.

Is government subsidy available?

Yes, various state and central government subsidies are available for institutional solar cooking systems. We assist clients with subsidy documentation and processing.
